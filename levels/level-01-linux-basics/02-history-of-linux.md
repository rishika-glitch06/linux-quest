# 👋 Let's Start

Imagine you're a computer science student in **1991**.

You want an operating system that you can explore, modify, and actually learn from.

But there's a problem...

Most operating systems at the time were either expensive, proprietary, or didn't let users see or modify the source code.

Now imagine thinking...

> **"What if I just build my own?"**

Sounds crazy, right?

Well...

That's exactly what happened.

---

# 👨‍💻 Meet Linus Torvalds

Linux was created by **Linus Torvalds**, a computer science student from Finland.

At that time, he was using a teaching operating system called **MINIX**, which was great for learning but had limitations.

Linus wanted something more powerful and more flexible.

So instead of waiting for someone else to build it...

He started building it himself.

What began as a personal project eventually became one of the most important software projects in history.

# 🌍 Why Did Linux Become So Popular?

Linux wasn't built by a huge company.

It grew because people loved the idea of collaboration.

Some reasons behind its popularity include:

- ✅ Free to use
- ✅ Open Source
- ✅ Highly Secure
- ✅ Stable
- ✅ Fast
- ✅ Customizable
- ✅ Supported by a global developer community

---

# 🐧 Why Is Linux Represented by a Penguin?

The Linux mascot is called **Tux**.

According to the popular story, Linus Torvalds was bitten by a penguin during a visit to a zoo.

Whether every detail of the story is perfectly accurate or not, Linus liked penguins, and the community eventually adopted **Tux** as Linux's mascot.

Today, the penguin is recognized around the world as the symbol of Linux.

Sometimes, the biggest innovations start as someone's side project.

# ❓Interview Questions

### Q1. Who created Linux?

**Answer:**
Linus Torvalds, a Finnish computer science student, created Linux in 1991.

---

### Q2. Why was Linux created?

**Answer:**
Linux was created because Linus wanted a free and flexible operating system that he could modify and learn from.

---

### Q3. What inspired Linux?

**Answer:**
Linux was inspired by UNIX and developed while Linus was using MINIX for learning.

---

### Q4. What is Tux?

**Answer:**
Tux is the official mascot of Linux.
